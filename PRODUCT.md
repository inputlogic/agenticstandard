# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

C-suite and VP-level decision makers at mid-to-large enterprises evaluating AI transformation. They've likely been burned by Big Four engagements that produced strategy decks and not much else. They're skeptical of consultants, tired of hype, and looking for someone who actually does the work. They arrive via referral, LinkedIn, or search, and they're comparing Agentic Standard against doing nothing, hiring internally, or engaging a larger firm.

## Product Purpose

Marketing site for Agentic Standard Inc., a consulting firm that deploys working agentic AI inside client organizations. The site's job is to build enough credibility and clarity that the right buyer books a 45-minute discovery call. Secondary job: give away the methodology so prospects can evaluate the firm's thinking before committing money.

## Brand Personality

Expert, direct, principled. Confident without arrogance. Opinionated without being preachy. The tone says "we've done this, here's what we know" rather than "we're the best." Writing is tight, declarative, and avoids consultant-speak. The firm leads with beliefs and gives the work away as proof of competence.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS marketing: gradient blobs, illustration packs, "trusted by" logo bars, bouncy animations, purple-to-blue hero gradients
- Big Four consulting sites: corporate blue, stock photography, meaningless buzzwords, walls of text about "digital transformation"
- AI startup hype pages: neon accents, dark mode with glowing orbs, particle effects, "powered by AI" badges, futuristic sans-serifs
- Template consulting: Squarespace-polished, interchangeable with any other boutique, no point of view

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** The site should feel like it was made by people who actually use AI well, not by people who talk about it. Craft signals competence.
2. **Show, don't sell.** Give away the methodology, the beliefs, the contract terms. Let the work speak. No testimonial carousels or trust badges needed.
3. **Respect the reader's time.** Every section earns its place. No filler intros, no restated headings, no "learn more" without substance behind it.
4. **Opinionated over safe.** The firm has a point of view. The design should too. Better to be memorable and polarizing than forgettable and polished.
5. **Warm authority.** Dark and serious enough to signal expertise, warm enough to signal that these are humans you'd want to work with.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline. Sufficient contrast ratios on all text. Keyboard navigable. Reduced-motion support for entrance animations. No accessibility-breaking patterns (text over complex backgrounds, tiny click targets, etc.).
